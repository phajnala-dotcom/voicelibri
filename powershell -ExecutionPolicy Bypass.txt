## CRITICAL RULES (MUST FOLLOW)

### General Principles
1. **NO WORD WASTE** - Every meaningful word from filename and metadata MUST be placed in most suitable field. Also from the file subfolder, if it is not duplicate or redndant generalized category  to those from filenama and from metadata
2. **NO DUPLICATES** - 1 mined word → 1 Excel cell only (never same word in Type AND Genre)
3. **GRAMMATICAL NORMALIZATION ONLY** - daylight→day OK, afternoon→day FORBIDDEN
4. **KEEP PLURALS IN SOURCES** - birds stays birds, don't change to bird
5. **Combine excess sources** - If >3 sources, put 2-3 in one cell separated by comma and space ", "

### Field-Specific Rules

**TYPE** (environment/setting - WHERE the sound exists, eventually sonical type):
- Nature sounds: forest, beach, ocean, river, mountain, cave, meadow, etc.
- Urban: city, street, traffic, market, town
- Interior: household, room, office, bar, restaurant, library
- Historical: castle, dungeon, ruins, palace
- Sci-fi: spacecraft, space, futuristic
- Combat: combat, military, battle (but NOT "war" as type)
- ⚠️ **HORROR RULE**: Horror is GENRE not TYPE (exception: use only if no other Type found)
- ⚠️ **CITY NAMES** are Location, not Type indicators
- ⚠️ **"village"** in nature context = nature/town, NOT city

**TEXTURE** (auditory characteristics - HOW it sounds):
- Impact: boom, crash, thud, impact, slam
- Sustained: drone, hum, buzz, rumble, hiss, crackle, 
- Movement: whoosh, swoosh, rush, pass, flyby
- Pitch: high-pitched (not just "high"), low-frequency (not just "low")
- Animal sounds: screech, growl, snarl, howl, chirp, squeak
- Metallic: clang, clank, chime, ring, rattle
- Percussive: percussive, rhythmic, pounding
- Design: cinematic, riser, stinger, transition, swell
- ⚠️ **"short"/"long"/"medium"** = audio duration, NOT texture - not in catalog, as already clear from track exact duration time

**GENRE** (literary/musical style):
- Literary (for AMB and SFX): horror, fantasy, sci-fi, historical, action, adventure, mystery, comedy, drama, crime, etc.
- Musical (for MUSIC): classical, electronic, folk, world, jazz, rock, orchestral
- ⚠️ Don't use "war" as genre - use "historical" or "action" instead

**ERA** (time period):
- prehistory, antiquity, medieval, modernity, historical, contemporary, future
- past era: prehistory, antiquity, medieval, modernity - these can be used only in case they are clearly identified (e.g. Beethoven - modernity, dinosaurs - prehistory, ancient Rome - antiquity), otherwise must be used Era: historical (universal for 2+ past eras or impossible to identify particular era, but clear that it was in past - not contemporary nor future) 
- ⚠️ **Nature types have NO era** (era-agnostic: forest, ocean, bird, etc.)
- ⚠️ **City/Technology/Urban = contemporary** always (except clearly historical technology (e.g. steam train), etc. or clearly city ambience from history)
- ⚠️ **technologies or invention clearly more advanced than contemporary = future** (e.g. spaceship, quantum teleportation, etc.

**MOOD** (emotional/psychological impact to listener - WHICH emotions/feelings it evokes). Semantic LLM AI inference required if missing
- Combat/fight/battle: Mood1=aggressive, Mood2=brutal (if blood/body damage etc.) OR tense (if threat only - SFX sound ofe arrow falling to water)
- Household/fireplace: Mood1=calm, Mood2=cosy
- Horror: Mood1=scary, Mood2=tense
- Nature peaceful: serene, tranquil, peaceful
- Nature dramatic: vast, powerful, majestic
- ⚠️ **Rich vocabulary** - fine detail differentiation anchored in imaginative reality
- ⚠️ **"Dark_Harmonics" / atmospheric drones** = scary/tense or mysterious/eerie, NOT aggressive
